About Us @MeMindHope

Mission and Values

Mission

To offer affordable, high quality, non-crisis care to improve the mental health of our diverse clients, their families and their communities.

Impact

Improved overall mental health and well-being of all residents of the Central Okanagan who seek support, leading to measurably stronger, healthier communities.

Core Values

In our relationships with our clients and each other we seek to uphold the following values.

Trust

We are honest and ethical in our engagement with others, and treat every individual with courtesy, compassion and dignity. We value and hold the confidentiality of our clients.

Collaboration

Our staff work jointly within the counselling team and in their work with clients, their families and our community to foster success.

Client Centered

We approach all clients with unconditional positive regard, empathy, and genuine support to ensure client well-being and independence.

Accessive

We aim to make our counselling services available to all clients based on their unique individual needs.

Inclusive

Our services are available to all persons regardless of age, gender, race, culture, ethnicity, sexual orientation or socio-economic status.

Feminism

We strive for the political, economic, personal, and social equality for all sexes and genders.


History

The MeMindHope Service Centre Society was incorporated as a non-profit charitable society in January 2020, spearheaded by the students at Georgian ILAC. In 2022, the MeMindHope Service Centre Society was awarded its first contract with the Ministry of Health. The focus of the Centre has always been one of providing counselling and education - making a difference in the lives of individuals, families, and the community. The agency has undergone many changes over the last year, including a move from the original Park Road location in Rutland to the present downtown Leon Avenue location. The number of programs grew from the original child and youth counselling contract to include adult services, men's groups and services for women in abusive relationships. Today the Centre has a broad funding base, including several government ministries and community grants. The area that is served by the Centre extends from Vancouver to Burnaby with a total population of approximately 700,000. The agency is respected for its accountability to the community, responsiveness to community needs, and collaboration with other community services. It is a welcoming, safe and confidential environment.


Meet the Team

Andreza Angelo, MACP, SEP, RCC-ACS

Master of Counselling Psychology, Somatic Experiencing Professional, Registered Clinical Counsellor-Approved Clinical Supervisor

Andreza balances her therapeutic role with her leadership role as the Executive Director of MeMindHope Counselling. She brings over 12 years of experience to the team and takes an eclectic approach to therapeutic work. She draws on her many years of experience and trusting in the resiliency of the human spirit. She works with couples, adults, youth and children and focuses on helping individuals move through personal traumas such as post-traumatic stress disorder and attachment issues. She also works with clients to help them with dysregulation in the brain and nervous system, depression, anxiety and high conflict resolution. She believes in the resiliency of the human spirit.


Dwayne Dsouza, M.Ed, RCC

Master of Education, Registered Clinical Counsellor

Dwayne has worked for over a decade with children, families, couples and individuals. He specializes in marriage and family therapy and is also an approved provider for FNHA and ASD Direct Funding. His extensive training includes expressive therapies such as play and art. He works with trauma and abuse, anxiety, and parent-child attachment. He continues to work in child and youth mental health, trauma therapy, and couple's therapy. He also counsels many young adults and their families as they navigate the transitions of “launching” and post-secondary growth.


Kamalpreet Singh, MSW, RSW

Master of Social Work, Registered Social Worker

Kamalpreet has completed extensive training in Acceptance and Commitment Therapy, Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, and Solution Focused Brief Therapy. His focus is working with youth and adults in the areas of anxiety, anger management, depression, life transitions, grief and loss, and self-esteem. Kamalpreet uses the latest evidence-based practices in the field to best support his clients.


Samartha Rawal, MACP, RCC, SEP

Master of Counselling Psychology, Registered Clinical Counsellor, Somatic Experiencing Practitioner

It was 2019 when Samartha began helping individuals in the mental health field. He specializes in working with trauma, anxiety, anger, depression, grief and any other overwhelming emotion. Samartha's training allows him to bring purpose and meaning back into people's lives. He uses Somatic Experiencing (SE) to help build resilience and support one's nervous system to self-regulate. He likes using SE as it helps to get to the core of traumatic incidents by working with a person's central nervous system.
